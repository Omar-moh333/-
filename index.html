About Kidney Failure

Kidney failure happens when the kidneys lose the ability to filter waste from the blood. It can be acute or chronic.

Symptoms

  • Fatigue
  • Swelling in legs and ankles
  • Nausea
  • Shortness of breath
  • Confusion
  • Low urine output

Treatment

Treatment includes dialysis, medications, and kidney transplant. Early diagnosis helps slow disease progression.
